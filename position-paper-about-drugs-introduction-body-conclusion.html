Answer:

purpose

The purpose of a position paper is to generate support on an issue. It describes the author’s position on an issue and the rational for that position and, in the same way that a research paper incorporates supportive evidence, is based on facts that provide a solid foundation for the author’s argument. It is a critical examination of a position using facts and inductive reasoning, which addresses both strengths and weaknesses of the author’s opinion.

Elements of the Position Paper

The classic position paper contains three main elements:

An Introduction, which identifies the issue that will be discussed and states the author’s position on that issue.

The Body of the paper, which contains the central argument and can be further broken up into three unique sections:

Background information

Evidence supporting the author’s position

A discussion of both sides of the issue, which addresses and refutes arguments that contradict the author’s position

A Conclusion, restating the key points and, where applicable, suggesting resolutions to the issue.

26. help po position papertopic: death penaltyIntroduction body conclusion​


Answer:

Introduction:

The death penalty, also known as capital punishment, is a highly controversial and divisive issue. The use of the death penalty as a form of punishment for certain crimes has been practiced throughout history and is still used in some countries today. Supporters of the death penalty argue that it serves as a deterrent and is a just punishment for heinous crimes, while opponents argue that it is inhumane, costly, and prone to error. In this position paper, I will argue that the death penalty should be abolished as it is a flawed and ineffective punishment.

Body:

One of the main arguments against the death penalty is that it is inhumane. The use of lethal injection, electrocution, hanging, and other methods of execution have been criticized for causing pain and suffering to the condemned. Additionally, the long wait on death row can cause psychological harm to the condemned and their families.

Another argument against the death penalty is that it is prone to error. The criminal justice system is not perfect and there have been numerous cases of innocent people being sentenced to death. The risk of executing an innocent person is too high and irreversible once it has been carried out.

The death penalty is also costly. The legal process for a death penalty case is much more expensive than that of a life imprisonment case. The cost of appeals and legal representation for the condemned is much higher, and this cost is passed on to taxpayers.

Furthermore, the death penalty does not effectively serve as a deterrent. Studies have shown that states with the death penalty do not have lower crime rates than states without it. Additionally, the majority of crimes that result in a death penalty sentence are not premeditated, which means that the perpetrator did not weigh the consequences of their actions before committing the crime.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, the death penalty is an inhumane, costly, and ineffective punishment. The risk of executing an innocent person, the long wait on death row, and the lack of evidence supporting its deterrent effect make it a flawed and unjust punishment. I believe that the death penalty should be abolished and replaced with more humane and effective alternatives, such as life imprisonment without the possibility of parole. This will ensure that justice is served while also protecting the rights of all citizens and ensuring the integrity of the criminal justice system.
