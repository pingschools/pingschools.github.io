1. C. Elective

3. C. MOU

Work Immersion is usually considered an elective subject in Senior High School in Philippines.The school personnel designated to supervise the learners during the Work Immersion is the Work Immersion Coordinator/Supervisor.Work immersion is a program in the Philippines that is part of the Senior High School curriculum. It is designed to give students practical, hands-on experience in a real-world work environment, and to expose them to different career paths and industries. During the work immersion program, students are placed in companies, organizations or government offices, where they work as interns, shadowing employees and learning about different job functions and responsibilities.The main goal of work immersion is to provide students with opportunities to apply the knowledge and skills they have acquired in the classroom in a real-world setting. It also aims to expose students to different work environments and cultures, to help them develop their problem-solving, communication, teamwork and other employability skills, and to give them a better understanding of the world of work.A Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) is a legal binding document that is signed by the two parties entering into partnership before implementing a project or program. MOU outlines the terms and objectives of the partnership, but it is not as legally binding as a Memorandum of Agreement (MOA). It's a document that outlines the general terms of an agreement and sets out the basic obligations and expectations of each party.

12. how would you describe an ideal work immersion supervisor


Answer:

In conclusion, an effective supervisor is one who offers leadership and brings the team together. It is someone with good communication skills, neutral, trustworthy, flexible and who knows how to delegate effectively. The leader should also be a learner and should treat their subordinates as partners.

*Honest and Empathetic,

Near-perfect bosses are honest about everything. They see secrets and lies as anunethical waste of time.

*Motivational and Nurturing

To grow in skills, you have to be motivated. Some high achievers are motivated internally by their own ambition, but even they need to be motivated by specific goals and accomplishments, not just by the desire to get ahead.

*Creative and Inspiring

Great bosses are creative. They devise different training methods and development goals for each employee. They don’t assume that the usual way is the best way.

*Guiding and Supportive,

The last thing best bosses do is boss people around. They get it that no one likes being bossed around and told what to do all the time. Instead of bossing, they guide.

14. An office found in most higher education institutions that can potentially assist students in their community immersion and where they plan to continue their volunteer work in the community.


The office of student affairs is an office found in most higher education institutions that can potentially assist students in their community immersion and where they plan to continue their volunteer work in the community.

Student Affairs is a division or department of service and support for the success of students in higher education. which aims to improve student development.

People who work in this division are called student professionals or practicioners. They are tasked with providing the best support and service for students, as well as encouraging positive student learning activities outside the classroom.

The roles of these working professionals range from service providers to senior leadership. The working staff also have diverse backgrounds and study programs. With that, they can complement each other in contributing to supporting students during their studies in college.

The following are five areas that are the responsibility of the Student Affairs Office :

-Admission and Guidance for Students

-Campus Life and Activities

-Difference

-Student Health and Wellness

-Alumni Relations

Each university has a number of service and administrative positions that vary according to the size of the educational institution concerned.

22. An office found in most higher education institutions that can potentially assist students in their community immersion and where they plan to continue their volunteer work in the community.


The name of the office is Community Development

Actually, the name of the office differs per higher education institution. However, it can be safely said that average higher education institution has a dedicated office whose aim is to assist students to be immersed in the community, as well as the preparation and training in becoming a volunteer. It is advised that college students participate in this kind of community to hone their soft skills.

Two examples of university with community development office:University of Santo Tomas has an office named Community Development, where its scope ranges from helping its students to learn and participate in community immersion as well as volunteering.University of Negros Occidental with its University Community Development Office (UCDO). its aim is similar to other community development office, which is to facilitate the students who want to take part in helping the community while they are still college students.

In addition, there is one similarity among all such higher institutions office across Philippines, it is that the majority of these offices in higher education institutions operate under the same program designed by the government: NSTP, which stands for National Service Training Program that covers how students can help in the community directly.
