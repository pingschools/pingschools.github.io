15. 5. What are the requirements needed by the living things in order to survive in a given en- vironment and when can you say that there is balanced in a particular environment?​


Answer:

In order to survive, animals need air, water, food, and shelter (protection from predators and the environment); plants need air, water, nutrients, and light. Every organism has its own way of making sure its basic needs are met.

THE NECESSITIES OF LIFE AND THE BALANCED ENVIRONMENT

Animals and plant, especially humans require air, water, food, and shelter (from predators and the environment) to survive, whereas plants require air, water, nutrients, and light. Every organism has its own method for ensuring that its fundamental requirements are satisfied. When differentiating between living and nonliving objects and identifying the requirements of living things, patterns in the natural world can be noticed and utilized as evidence. All living creatures require air, water, and food to exist. Plants and other animals offer food for animals, which gives them the energy they need to move and develop. The habitat of an animal must offer these fundamental necessities (air, water, and food), as well as protection from predators and severe weather. Plants require air, water, and food as well, but they lack mouths to breathe, drink, and consume. Plants use small pores on the underside of their leaves to "breathe" air. Plants use their roots to "drink" water and "eat" their food (nutrients) from the soil. They also use sunlight and energy from the sun to create food in their leaves, which aids their growth.

A sustainable environment is defined by a balanced ecology. It is made up of animals, plants, microbes, and other creatures that are interdependent on one another and their environment. These ecosystems are known for their efficient energy and material cycling. It also demonstrates interdependence between primary producers and predators.
