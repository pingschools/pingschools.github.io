28. Write a two-stanza poem using visual imagery. Describe your surrounding that mostly influences your line of thought. ​


Winter and me

A rainy new year's eve that robs my moods..

A chirp of such cricket that embezzles my ears..

A cool windy wind that tries to make me chills..

These thoughts hold me a loner..

How much does another offer after tonight..

With an alluring smiling face of a sunrise..

Wishing for a quiet, vivid green world..

A pleasant day is when the summer changes the weather..

Time

Tick tock...tick tock...

Life is ticking down on your internal clock.

The recollection that enjoys as if it took place yesterday

turn to flickers of memories that seem to wither away.

Folks you once knew

wander by with no clue.

The moments we always had in common

remain as if you were even there.

Decades fly...friends die...

but you never know when you`ll tell your last goodbye.

Oh, how I wish I could travel back in time,

keep wasting it with near and dear ones and treasure what used to be mine.

Or even go back even more,

being such a kid in a candy store.

How I appreciate the way I used to feel

on Xmas eve day while Santa was real.

But back to reality...back to today,

family is limited and photographs begin to drift away.

Tick tock...tick tock...

I hoped I could cast the time on this clock.

Further explanation:

Couplets are two-line stanzas. Couplets typically rhyme, but this is not a requirement. In poetry, a stanza is a collection of lines that are typically divided by a blank line. Couplets, from the Old French term cople, which means two, are the name given to 2-line stanzas.

What is an example of a stanza in a poem?

Shakespeare's most well-known sonnet could serve as an illustration of a stanza in a poem. The sonnet is composed of three quatrains, an ABAB, CDCD, EFEF rhyme pattern, and a closed couplet of GG.

How do you compose a poem using two stanzas?

Vary your rhyme systems. A stanza may have five lines that rhyme ABABC (the first and third lines rhyme, the second and fourth lines rhyme, and the fifth line doesn't rhyme with any of the others), plus an additional line. The second stanza might have six lines or follow the same format.
