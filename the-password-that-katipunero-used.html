What are passwords used for?

Passwords provide the first line of defense against unauthorized access to your computer and personal information. The stronger your password, the more protected your computer will be from hackers and malicious software. You should maintain strong passwords for all accounts on your computer

Which technique is mostly used in password authentication?Hashing your passwords.

Hashing your passwords translates them into a random set of characters. Once the data has been hashed, it's extremely difficult to decode the information without the key. This is especially useful for passwords because they don't need to be read back

Where are passwords used?

A password is a string of characters used to verify the identity of a user during the authentication process. Passwords are typically used in tandem with a username; they are designed to be known only to the user and allow that user to gain access to a device, application or website.

8. Which of the following is safe practice? Select one: a. Use a combination of letters, numbers, and symbols as a password b. Save your password in every device that you use c. Use your mom's birthday as a password so it's easy to remember d. Write your password in a piece of paper so you won't forget it


The answer is a. Use a combination of letters, numbers, and symbols as a password. Using strong passwords is important in keeping confidential details, and having personal privacy. Therefore, people must use safe practices in making passwords. An example of a safe practice is using a combination of letters, numbers, and symbols as a password.

Why do People Need to Follow Safe Practices when Creating Passwords

Safe practices must be observed by people when creating passwords because of these two main reasons:

to keep confidential and personal information to protect personal privacy against intruders Examples of Safe Practices when Creating Passwords

Aside from using a combination of letters, numbers, and symbols as a password, here are other safe practices when creating passwords:

Do not save your password in every device so that other people who may have access to your device will not be able to open your accounts. Do not use basic and obvious information such as your mom's birthday, your birthday, phone name, names of family members, etc. Do not write password in a piece of paper as other people might find that paper or information. Avoid using same passwords for different accounts. Avoid using passwords using adjacent keys such as "qwerty" or "1234".
