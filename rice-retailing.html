11. A retailer wishes to mix rice selling at $32 per kg. with rice selling at $35 per kg so as to make a mixture of 48 kg. worth $33 per kg. How many kilograms of each kind must be used?​


Answer:

Therefore, the retailer should mix 32 kg of rice selling at $32 per kg with 16 kg of rice selling at $35 per kg to obtain 48 kg of mixture at $33 per kg.

Step-by-step explanation:

Let x be the amount of rice at $32 per kg that the retailer needs to mix, and y be the amount of rice at $35 per kg. We want to find x and y such that:

•The total amount of rice is 48 kg: x + y = 48

•The total cost of the mixture is $33 per kg: 32x + 35y = 33(48)

We can use these two equations to solve for x and y. Here's how:

1. Solve for one variable in terms of the other:

x + y = 48

y = 48 - x

2. Substitute this expression for y into the second equation:

32x + 35y = 33(48)

32x + 35(48 - x) = 1584

32x + 1680 - 35x = 1584

-3x = -96

x = 32

3. Use this value of x to find y:

y = 48 - x

y = 48 - 32

y = 16
