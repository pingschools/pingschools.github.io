Activity 2: Kinetic and Potential Energy

1. Given:

 mass of truck= 2000kg

  velocity of truck = 0 m/s

  mass of bike + biker = 60kg

  velocity of bike + biker = 5m/s

The bike and biker has greater kinetic energy than the truck. Kinetic energy is the energy in motion. Mathematically, kinetic energy is represented by the formula, KE = [tex]\frac{1}{2}[/tex] m[tex]v^{2}[/tex]. It means that kinetic energy is directly proportional to the object's mass and the square of the velocity. The faster the object moves, the greater the kinetic energy. The truck is parked on a curb, thus, has zero velocity. Since the truck is at rest, it will also have no kinetic energy. In contrast to the bike and biker that is in motion thus possesses kinetic energy. Using the formula, the truck will have zero kinetic energy while the bike and biker will have a kinetic energy of 750 J.

2. Given:

   mass of pig = 50 kg

   height = 0 m

   mass of banana = 50 kg

   height = 10m

   g = 9.8 m/[tex]s^{2}[/tex]

Between the banana and pig, the banana will have greater potential energy. Even though the pig and banana have the same mass, the position of the two objects caused the difference in their potential energies. Potential energy is energy at rest or stored energy. It is affected by the mass, height, and pull of gravity. Specifically, potential energy is directly proportional to mass and height. The pull of gravity on earth is constant and the acceleration due to the gravity (g) is 9.8 m/[tex]s^{2}[/tex]. Potential energy is represented by the formula, PE =mgh. In the question, the pig is lying on the floor, thus having a zero height. Substituting the values to the formula of potential energy, the pig has zero potential energy. On the other hand, the banana is hanging 10 m from the ground. Substituting the values to the formula of the potential energy, the banana will have a potential energy of 4, 900 J.
