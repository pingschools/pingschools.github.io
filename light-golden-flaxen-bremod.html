24. Basic body color for horses is influenced by several genes, on of which has several different alleles. Two of these alleles, the chestnut (dark brown) allele and a diluting (pale cream) allele (often incorrectly called ‘albino’), display incomplete dominance. A horse heterozygous for these two alleles is a palomino (golden body color with flaxen mane and tail). Is it possible to produce a herd of pure breeding palomino horses? Why or why not? Work the Punnett’s square for mating a palomino to a palomino and predict the phenotypic ratio among their offspring.


Answer:

No, it is not possible to produce a herd of pure breeding palomino horses. Palomino is a phenotype resulting from the heterozygous expression of chestnut and diluting alleles, which means that a palomino horse has one of each allele (C+cch). When two palomino horses are mated, there is a 50% chance of producing a chestnut offspring (Cc) and a 50% chance of producing a palomino offspring (Cc). The Punnett square would look like this:

| Cc | Cc

--|----|----

cch| Cc | Cc

cch| Cc | Cc

The phenotypic ratio among the offspring would be 50% chestnut and 50% palomino.
