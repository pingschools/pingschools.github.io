What are PECs or Personal Entrepreneurial Competencies?

These are one's qualities, characteristics, skills, abilities, knowledge and behavior that a potential businessman must exhibit to become successful. But what are these? There are three clusters, namely:  achievement, planning and power. Under achievement cluster, it requires you to be committed and a risk taker. If you are not committed and persistent, you can easily give up on the business. Also, you need to be able to take risks and seek out solutions when others see a problem.  

The planning cluster involves you  being goal oriented and working on evidenced based decisions. If you only work on whims and non-evidence based actions, then it will be difficult.  

Power cluster refers to being persuasive and self confident. Usually introverts are not that good at businesses (Not all the time!). BUt when proper persuasion and networking, it can be done.

17. Explain the importance of assessing one’s PECs before engaging in a particular entrepreneurial activity.


Importance of Assessing One’s PECs:

       Before engaging in any entrepreneurial activity, it is necessary to assess the individual’s personal entrepreneurial competencies. Anybody who wishes to get engaged or venture with any form of business should willingly participate or perform such assessment as a form of self – evaluation. The following summarizes the reasons for such assessment:

Serves as useful tool in assessing one’s strength and weaknesses Identify several competencies that need consideration and practice to be further developed. Means of turning behaviors into habits and apply them in developing their business. Serve as means to enhance production and competition towards an economic development advantage. Identify areas for development, growth, and improvement. Be able to align one’s PECs according to his business or career choice. Plan the course of action that guarantees success within the business or career choice.

Personal Entrepreneurial Competencies can be a useful tool in assessing one’s strength and weaknesses since it considers areas such as attributes, characteristics, lifestyles, skills, and traits. Having positive and healthy attributes, characteristics, lifestyles, skills, and traits are definitely what makes a strong personality. However, having weaknesses and recognizing them is a different story. Addressing these weaknesses properly can turn them into strengths also.

There are several competencies that need to be considered and practiced in order to be further developed. For instance, major adjustments are needed to be done within one’s lifestyle as he ventures into a particular business of his choice. These include adjusting expenses and making more projects that would generate more incomes.

Turning behaviors into habits and applying then in order to develop their business is a good business practice however; these behaviors must be good enough to make things work for the good of the business.

Enhancing production and competition towards economic development advantage can start with assessing one’s personal entrepreneurial competencies. Being productive and competitive is usually triggered by one’s strength and edges over other entrepreneurs.

Areas for development, growth, and improvement can be in terms of one’s attributes, characteristics, lifestyles, skills, and traits. An entrepreneur may possess all the necessary traits and attributes but do not have a healthy and positive lifestyle which he needs to improve to make everything work successfully.

Aligning one’s PEC’s according to his preferred career and business needs careful assessment of his strengths and weaknesses. When things are aligned negative elements are eliminated and issues are easily addressed.

To be able to plan the course of action that should be taken only guarantees success when they are carefully planned and studied including the resources that are to be utilized in operating the business. This is also the best way of exhibiting how to be responsible with the decisions made concerning the business.

27. Explain the importance of assessing ones PECs before engaging in a particular entrepreneural activity?


Assessing of personal entrepreneur competence (PEC) is very important because it is related to the success or failure of their business. Because an entrepreneur is a person who directly manages a business, makes decisions, motivates and controls employees, negotiates with customers, manages and controls money, and so on.

--------------

The characteristics of entrepreneurs are:

1. Self-confidence (trust, independence, steady personality, optimism)

The characteristics of a person's maturity are that he is not dependent on others, he has a high sense of responsibility, is objective and critical. Not only does he absorb other people's opinions or opinions, but he considers them critically. Emotionally, it can be said that he is stable, not easily offended and has a high social level as well as being a helper and close to Khaliq the creator.

2. Task and result oriented (need or thirst for achievement, profit or result oriented, diligent and steadfast, determination to work hard, energetic motivation, full of initiative)

These characteristics do not prioritize prestige first, then achievement will be followed, but he is infatuated with achievement and then after he is successful, his prestige will increase. Various motivations will emerge in business if we try to get rid of prestige.

3. Risk taking (ability to take reasonable risks)

Someone who likes to take risks will make a good contribution if he is brought into entrepreneurship to get used to the risks and challenges, such as price competition up and down, goods not selling, and business competition. However, all these challenges must be faced with full calculation.

4. Leadership (able to lead, can get along with others, respond to suggestions and criticisms)

The nature of leadership does exist within each individual. A good leader must be responsive and willing to accept criticism from subordinates.

5. Originality (innovative, creative, flexible)

The nature of originality is not following others, but having their own opinion, having original ideas, and having the ability to do something. Original does not mean completely new, but the product reflects the result of a new combination or reintegration of existing components, thus giving birth to something new.

6. Future-oriented (Learn from past experiences and always have a view for progress and goal achievement)

An entrepreneur must have a perspective, have a vision for the future, therefore continuity must be maintained and the view must be aimed far ahead. To face the foresight, an entrepreneur will develop a careful planning and strategy, so that the steps that will be implemented are clear.

Entrepreneurial spirit, attitude and behavior have the following characteristics:

Full of confidence, with indicators full of confidence, optimism, discipline, commitment and responsibility;Have the initiative, with indicators full of energy, nimble in action and active;Have achievement motives with results-oriented and forward-looking indicators;Have a leadership spirit with indicators of daring to be different, trustworthy and tough in acting; andDare to take calculated risks.

Confidence is translated into the character of independence, individuality and optimism. The characteristics of the need for achievement include profit-oriented character, perseverance and fortitude, determination and hard work, great motivation, energy and initiative. The ability to take risks means that you like challenges. Acting as a leader means being able to get along with other people (subordinates), responding to suggestions and criticisms, being innovative, flexible, having many sources, being versatile and knowing a lot. In addition, entrepreneurs have foresight and an advanced perspective.
