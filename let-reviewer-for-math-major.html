Let Us Review What are the differences of Major and Minor scale?

ANSWER:

A major scale is a scale in which the third scale degree (the mediant) is a major third above the tonic note. In a minor scale, the third degree is a minor third above the tonic. Similarly, in a major triad or major seventh chord, the third is a major third above the chord's root.

2. In certain math 17 class in UP, 30% are computer science mahors, 20% are industrial engineering majors, and the rest are chemistry majors. Of there are 30 students in the class majoring in chemistry, how many students are majoring in industrial engineering?


Note:  Math17 is code for Math subject in UP (Algebra and Trigo), hence, 17 is not a given value.

Find the total number of students, x:

[100% - (30% + 20%)](x) = 30
50%(x) = 30
0.5x/0.5 = 30/0.5
x = 60   ⇒  Total number of students in Math17 class

Find the number of students majoring in Industrial Engineering:

Number of IE major students = 0.20 (60)
Number of IE major students = 12

ANSWER: 12 students in Math17 class are majoring in Industrial Engineering
