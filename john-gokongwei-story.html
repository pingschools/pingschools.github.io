Answer:

From peanuts to billions

Based in Cebu, he started out as an entrepreneur at the tender age of 13, forced to earn money for his family after the untimely passing of his father. He started very small, selling fried peanuts, and worked his way up to selling thread, soap, and candles on a bicycle.

13. Theo bought 150 shares of Gokongwei Group of Companies (GGC) at ₱15,000 per share. GGC paid ₱650 per share of stocks last year. Theo decided to hold the stock for the next five years expecting a constant growth of 3.5% in dividend payments. What are the expected amounts of dividends in the next five years? ​


To calculate the expected amounts of dividends in the next five years for Theo's GGC shares, we can use the formula for compound interest:

A = P(1 + r)^n

Where:

A = the future value (the expected amount of dividends)

P = the initial investment (the amount of dividends paid last year)

r = the growth rate (3.5% in this case)

n = the number of years (5 in this case)

So, we can plug in the known values:

A = 650(1 + 0.035)^5

A = 650(1.035)^5

A = 650(1.1937)

A = 781.955

So, the expected amount of dividends in the next five years is approximately ₱781.955 per share. Since Theo has 150 shares, the total expected dividends for the next five years would be 150 x 781.955 = ₱117293.25.

30. how ' john gokongwei BECAME SUCCESSFUL WITH THEIR CHOSEN FIELD AS ENTREPRENEUR?​


Answer:

John Robinson Lim Gokongwei Jr. was a Filipino billionaire businessman and philanthropist. He had holdings in telecommunications, financial services, petrochemicals, power generation, aviation, food, beverage, and livestock farming.

From living in a stylish home, 15-year-old John became a market vendor. He sold peanuts and just whatever he could make money out of. Even with little cash, he allotted some to send to his family in China. He was eventually able to buy a bicycle which enabled him to sell goods in nearby towns.

John Gokongwei Jr. died in November 2019 at the age of 93. He was the third-richest person in the Philippines. He was the founder of JG Summit which has interests in airlines, telecom, banking, food, power, and property. His brother James Go chairs JG Summit.
