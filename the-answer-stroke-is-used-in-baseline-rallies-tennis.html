14. what is rally in tennis​


Answer:

A rally in tennis is a collective name given to a sequence of back and forth shots between players, within a point. A rally starts with the serve and the return of the serve, followed by continuous return shots until a point is scored which ends the rally.

This is the stroke is used in baseline railles

Ground Stroke: A ground stroke can be performed with either a forehand or a backhand grip.  Typically a ground stroke is used when the player is only a few feet away from the baseline. Lob: The lob stroke is when the player hits the ball well above the head of the opponent  so that the ball lands deep in the court towards the baseline with a forehand or a backhand grip.  A lob is a great shot for defensive scenarios, or a player's opponent is likely to close the net. A high lob can be used to buy a player's time to allow them to move back to position.

Other tennis Strokes  

Serve: To serve, the player throws the ball up in the air and uses an overhead stroke  to get the ball over the net and into the service box of the opposing player.  A serve is used to start each  point of the game before the ball bounces.  The player is given two chances to hit the ball over the  net and into the service box on the opposite side of the court.  The server can be hit behind the baseline and  diagonally hit the service box of the other player.

Forehand Ground Stroke: The forehand ground stroke is the easiest  

and most commonly the first stroke learned in tennis. To perform a ground strike, the player will swing the  tennis racket across their body on the same side as their dominating hand to hit the ball.

Backhand Ground Stroke: To perform a backhand strike, the player will swing their racket  away from their body on the opposite side of the hand.

Volley: A volley can be used to hit the ball while the player is approaching the net or on the net.  A volley can be used to hit the ball at any angle by getting closer to the net during the stroke.  To perform a volley, the stroke must use the overhead motion and hit the ball before it bounces.

Once a player has a better understanding of the different types of tennis strokes, they can continue to  improve their ability to play. It is important to ensure that proper body mechanics are used to  prevent tennis elbow pain and other possible injuries when performing these strokes.
