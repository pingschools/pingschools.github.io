Answer:

Counts 1, 2, 3. Starting position: R (L) foot in fifth position in front, heels slightly raised or with the leading foot flat on the floor. Execute tiny steps sideward right (left) as many times as necessary. In ¾ time take one step for every count. In 2/4 time it maybe 2 steps for every count.

21. which of the following dance steps has step pattern of step, close, step and a counting of 1 2 3 A.waltz B.cross-waltz C.Mazurka D.Redoba


Which of the following dance steps has step pattern of step, close, step and a counting of 1 2 3 A.waltz B.cross-waltz C.Mazurka D. Redoba

The answer is A. Here are the explanation.

Cross Waltz-count to one scale 1, 2, 3.Step R across L in front and raise the L foot slightly backwards  (ct.1), step L near to R in rear (ct. 2), step R forward (ct. 3). On ct. 1. Knees are slightly bent.  Step on rear foot ball at ct. 2. Typically this step is taken in either the forward or the sideward direction.  3/4 Signing time

Waltz balance-count 1, 2, 3 to calculate. Step R forward (ct. 1), near L to R in third rear or first place  and lift both heels (ct. 2), lower heels with body weight on R foot (ct. 3). This can happen in any direction.3/4 Signing time

Waltz step – Count 1, 2, 3 to the measure Step R sideward (ct. 1) then close L to R (ct. 2) and step R to position  (ct. 3) 3/4 Time signature.

In performing the galop, the man put his right hand around his partner's waist and with his left hand held her right hand; the couple danced the galop's simple step side by side—slide (or glide), close (chassé), slide—around the ballroom to music in 2/4 time. 26. which of the following dance step has a step pattern of step close step and counting of 1 2 3 4 5​


Answer:

The waltz

Explanation:

The dance step that has a step pattern of "step, close, step" and counting of "1, 2, 3, 4, 5" is likely to be the Waltz. The Waltz is a ballroom dance that originated in the late 18th century in Austria and Germany. It is a smooth and elegant dance that is danced in 3/4 time and is characterized by a step, close, step pattern and a slow, steady rhythm. The dance is typically counted as "1, 2, 3" with the steps taken on the first, second, and third beats of the music, respectively.
