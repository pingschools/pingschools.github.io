Communication can be affected when conflicting or false information is conveyed through media channels with an obvious political slant or bias or a campaign of willful disinformation. It can also be affected by a positive message allowing the receiver to gather their own facts and make an informed decision regarding a particular issue. It can also help shape opinion and enhance public debate when done properly. The media carries much responsibility for presenting the factually correct information to the masses. When that information is transmitted to a select group, the message can become distorted especially if “opinion programming” is taking an opposing claim and distorting those facts in order to present their position on an issue. Channels of communication can also become clogged with useless and unnecessary information leading to a particular message from being received properly, twisted factually or becoming propaganda.
