An electromagnetic wave is characterized by its intensity and the frequency of the time variation of the electric and magnetic fields. In modern quantum theory, electromagnetic radiation is the flow of photons through space. Photons are packets of energy hν that always move with the speed of light.  Symbol h is Planck’s constant, on the other hand the value of ν is the same as that of the frequency of the electromagnetic wave.

Electromagnetic radiation is made when an atom absorbs energy. Absorbed energy makes one or more electrons to change their locale within the atom. It can make the living thing vomit, have hair loss or even damage blood cells. Electro magnetic radiation can damage nature terribly, since a person's body temperature can be raised which could result in death if exposed. Some species can be sensitive to electromagnetic radiation. Species like migratory birds, bats, and certain fish and insects.
