28. The SMB partnership earned a net profit of 28,000. The partners jed, joshua, janine and john share profits and losses: 1/4 for jed, 2/8 for joshua, 2/6 for janine and the rest for john. compute for the share of the partners in the net profit.


Answer:

Jed's shares = 7,000

Joshua's shares = 7,000

Janine's shares = 9,333.33

John's shares = 4,666.67

Step-by-step explanation:

To answer the question, we need to multiply each person's fraction with the net profit.

1) Jed's shares = [tex]\frac{1}{4} (28,000)[/tex]

[tex]= \frac{28,000}{4}[/tex]

= 7,000

2) Joshua's shares = [tex]\frac{2}{8} (28,000)[/tex]

[tex]= \frac{1}{4} (28,000)[/tex]

[tex]= \frac{28,000}{4}[/tex]

= 7,000

3) Janine's shares = [tex]\frac{2}{6} (28,000)[/tex]

[tex]= \frac{1}{3} (28,000)[/tex]

[tex]= \frac{28,000}{3}[/tex]

≈ 9,333.33

4) John's shares = the rest of what is left

[tex]= 28,000 - (7,000 + 7,000 + 9,333.33)[/tex]

[tex]= 28,000 - 23,333.33[/tex]

= 4,666.67
