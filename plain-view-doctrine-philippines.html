1. scenario of plain view doctrine​


Answer:

The plain view doctrine allows a police officer to seize objects not described in a warrant when executing a lawful search or seizure if he observes the object in plain view and has probable cause to believe that it is connected with criminal activities.  The incriminating character of the object should be immediately identifiable.  Evidence in a container can not possibly be identified by plain observation or touch and therefore should not be seized unless it is listed in the warrant.

17. Meaning of doctrine of dependent relative revocation philippines


Dependent Relative Revocation

In Philippine laws, the meaning of Doctrine of Dependent Relative Revocation is that in any case that the new created will is ineffective or cannot be used, the previous published will could be used as a substitute. A fair example of that is when someone makes a will in hopes of creating a new will which would ultimately replace the previous one, but somehow, the drafted new will is ineffective or cannot be used, the doctrine allows the destroyed or former will to be "revived" and takes its place instead.
