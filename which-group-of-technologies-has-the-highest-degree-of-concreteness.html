16. how population and technology affect and influence the global economy of our country.cite concrete evidences 200 words essay ​


Answer:

Population and technology have a significant impact on the global economy of a country. The increase in population leads to an increase in demand for goods and services, which can create economic growth. Additionally, advances in technology can improve productivity, making it easier for businesses to produce goods and services, and compete in the global market.

In the Philippines, the population has been steadily increasing over the years, and this has created opportunities for economic growth. The Philippine Statistics Authority reported that the country's population reached 109 million in 2021, which is expected to grow even further in the coming years. This increase in population means more consumers, and thus a greater demand for goods and services.

On the other hand, technology has also played a significant role in the growth of the Philippine economy. The country has a well-developed IT and business process outsourcing (BPO) industry, which has attracted numerous multinational corporations to set up shop in the country. This industry has created numerous job opportunities and has contributed significantly to the country's GDP.

Moreover, technological advancements in the agricultural sector have improved crop yields, making it possible for the Philippines to export agricultural products to other countries. The country's electronics industry is also growing, with electronics exports reaching USD 3.98 billion in 2020.

However, these factors also have negative impacts on the global economy. The increase in population can lead to resource depletion and environmental degradation, which can harm the country's natural resources and ecosystems. Additionally, while technology can create jobs, it can also lead to job loss in certain industries due to automation.

In conclusion, population growth and technological advancements can significantly impact the global economy of a country, including the Philippines. While these factors can contribute to economic growth, they also need to be managed properly to avoid negative impacts on the environment and employment opportunities. The government must take a strategic approach to manage these factors and ensure that the benefits of economic growth are distributed equitably across society.

18. how are minority groups discriminated on the philippines society? cite concrete examples.​


Answer:

Discrimination against minority groups in the Philippines is a complex issue that is rooted in historical and societal factors. Here are some examples of how certain minority groups are discriminated against:

Indigenous peoples (IPs) or Lumad: They often face discrimination and marginalization from the mainstream society. They are often displaced from their ancestral lands due to mining, logging, and other development projects. They also have limited access to basic services such as education and health care.
LGBTQ+ community: Despite recent advancements in terms of legislation, members of the LGBTQ+ community still face discrimination and harassment, particularly in rural areas. Transgender individuals are particularly vulnerable to violence and discrimination.
Muslims: Members of the Muslim community often face discrimination and prejudice due to their religion and cultural practices. They are often stereotyped as terrorists and are subject to racial profiling.
People with disabilities (PWDs): They face various forms of discrimination, including limited access to education, employment, and public spaces. PWDs are often excluded from mainstream society, and their rights and welfare are not fully recognized.

These are just some examples of how minority groups are discriminated against in the Philippines. It is important to note that discrimination can take many forms and can be subtle or overt. Addressing discrimination requires a concerted effort from various stakeholders, including government agencies, civil society organizations, and the general public.
