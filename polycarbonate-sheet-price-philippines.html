3. Polycarbonate plastic has a density of 1.2 g/cm³. A tray is constructed from a sheet of polycarbonate with a thickness of 0.75 cm and measures 36.0 cm by 26.0 cm. What is the mass of the tray?​


Answer:

The volume of the tray can be calculated by multiplying its length, width, and thickness:

Volume 36.0 cm x 26.0 cm x 0.75 cm = 702.0 cm³

The density of polycarbonate is 1.2 g/cm³, which means that every cubic centimeter of polycarbonate has a mass of 1.2 grams. Therefore, the mass of the tray can be calculated by multiplying its volume by the density:

Mass = Volume x Density

Mass = 702.0 cm³ x 1.2 g/cm³

Mass = 842.4 grams

Therefore, the mass of the tray is 842.4 grams, or 0.8424 kilograms (since there are 1000 grams in a kilogram).

6. Describe the enabling materials property of each of the following and why is this so: (a) steel for I-beams in skyscrapers, (b) a cobalt chrome molybdenum alloy for hip implants, © polycarbonate for eyeglass lenses, and (d) bronze for artistic castings.


Describe the enabling materials property of each of the following and why is this so:

(a) Steel for I-beams in skyscrapers - Because of its form, steel I-beams are trusted to form the skeleton of many skyscrapers. It is the most cost-effective material to use in such projects. Its density and design make it a much lighter material than even wood since it would take more wooden material to fill the empty spaces allowed with the metal I-beams to achieve the same strength.

(b) Cobalt chrome molybdenum alloy for hip implants - This material is resistant to corrosion, it also does not give in easily to metal stress. It's inherent strength and hardness makes it a reliable hip implant material.

(c) Polycarbonate for eyeglass lenses - compared to real glass which is much heavier, polycarbonate is lighter and far more resistant to impact. Wearing them both and comparing the two will almost surely change the mind of someone using real glasses on their eyewear.

(d) Bronze for artistic castings - Bronze is one of those metals that becomes more beautiful as it ages. Such a quality fits well with sculptures which are meant to be admired forever. Working with bronze enables the expression of clean lines, curves, and tiny crevices which darken in time, adding character to the sculpture.

It is resistant to corrosion and is very strong, characteristics that are needed when you want to immortalize a figure.

  Polyurethane refers to a thermoplastic polymer containing the group NHCOO: used for padding and insulation in furniture, clothing, and packaging, and in the manufacture of resins for adhesives, elastomers, and fillers while Polycarbonate is a synthetic thermoplastic resin, a linear polymer of carbonic acid, used for molded products, films, and nonbreakable windows.
