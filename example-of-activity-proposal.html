Simple Present Tense

A. It usually expresses statements which is meant to be true

Example: The ocean is deep.

B. It shows a habit or a usual activity with adverbs like usually, every day, often, etc.

Example: I attend mass every Sunday.

C. It is used to give a commentary about an action that is currently happening.

Example: The traffic light turns green and the drivers move their cars immediately.

D. It makes the statement true in the future

Example: The bus leaves the station at exact noon.

AgreementsThe subject and the verb should agree in number. The rule says that singular subjects take singular verb, while plural subjects takes plural verbs.

a. SingularA subject is singular if refers to only one person. A group can also be a singular subject if it is meant as a whole in the sentence.

A verb is singular if it has s or es to the base word.

Example: The teacher teaches the lesson.

Here, the teacher is the singular subject so it will take the singular verb teaches.

b. PluralA subject is plural if it has two or more people in the sentence joined by the conjunction and. It is also plural if the subject is a group that is not meant as a whole in the sentence. A verb if plural if it doesn't have s or es to the base word.

Example: The teachers teach the lesson.

Here, the subject teachers becomes plural by adding s to the base word. It also takes the plural verb teach without any s or es to the base word.
