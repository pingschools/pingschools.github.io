PLAGIARISM

Plagiarism is made easier with technology and the Internet. Everyone’s written work is available on the world wide web. People just copy and paste whatever they see, and present it as their own work, which is plagiarism by definition. Also because of this, people get less creative. No more need for critical or imaginative thinking.

An example is when a student is tasked to make a discussion paper. Students go to their phones or laptops and type in the keywords on the Internet. The student just reads minimally and copy what seems right. There is the copying of a written work -- no thinking nor creativity, imagination, and ingenuity needed.  

Plagiarism is simply the copying of another person’s work and presenting it as your own. Plagiarism is illegal under the Intellectual Property Code, a special law, which punishes it with imprisonment and fine. Plagiarism is a form of cheating as it puts a person on top without working hard.
