23. do's and don't cleaning the electric flat iron​


Five do's cleaning the electric flat iron

1. Clean the soleplate of an iron with baking soda and water.

This procedure is excellent for dissolving any gunk that has taken up residence on your iron! To make a paste, simply combine some baking soda with a small amount of water.

Scrub the soleplate of the iron with this mixture using a soft brush or towel. Simply wipe it away with water. Before using the iron, make sure the paste is entirely removed and that it is absolutely dry.

2. A newspaper can be used.

To protect your hands from the heat, set the iron to the maximum setting and put on oven mittens. Scrunch up some newspaper and scrape the soleplate of the iron with it.

3. Another method for cleaning an iron is to use an acetone nail polish remover.

This is a fantastic way to dissolve and wipe away the dirtiness from an iron! Turn on the iron and wait for it to heat up. Using acetone nail polish remover, soak a cotton ball.

Quickly dab the moistened cotton ball on the hot surface while wearing oven mittens. Acetone nail polish remover is quick to evaporate, but it performs a fantastic job of removing any dirt! Wipe the area clean with water and a cloth once you're done.

4. Scrub the mess away with kitchen sponges and scrubbers!

Scrub the iron's surface with soap and water using a non-scratch kitchen sponge or scrubber.

28. an electric flat iron draws a current of 4.2 amperes when it is converted toba 220 volt line circuit. what is the power of the electric flat iron?​


Answer:

P=924 Watts

Solution:

We can solve the problem using 2 formula.

Using Ohm's Law:

In order to get the power, we need to identify what is the value of resistance.

R = V ÷ I

=220V ÷ 4.2A

R= 52.38 Ohms.

We can now solve the value of Power using:

P=I²×R

=(4.2)²×52.38

P=923.98 or 924 Watts.

Using another formula:

P=I×E

=4.2A×220V

P=924 Watts.
