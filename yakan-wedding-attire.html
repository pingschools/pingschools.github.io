28. Choose one of the art/craft during Yakan weddings and describe its use of elements and principles of art. (line, color, shape, pattern, etc.)


Answer:

One of the art/craft during Yakan weddings is the weaving of the traditional Yakan cloth, known as "sina-sina." The use of elements and principles of art in this craft is notable.

Firstly, the weaving process incorporates intricate patterns and designs, which display the principle of pattern. The weavers carefully choose the colors of their thread and weave them together to create unique and beautiful designs. The geometric patterns created by the weavers symbolize the essence of the Yakan culture, and the vivid colors used represent the vibrant life force of the community.

The use of color in the Yakan cloth is also prominent, highlighting the use of the principle of color. The bright and bold hues used represent joy, fertility, and vitality in Yakan culture. The colors used in the Yakan cloth are derived from organic materials, such as roots and leaves of plants and vegetables, which are an essential part of the Yakan wedding rituals. The use of natural color reflects the culture's connection to the earth, and its respect for the environment.

In addition, the weavers employ the principle of line, incorporating different types of lines in their designs. The straight lines depict order and consistency, while the curved lines symbolize harmony and balance. The weavers also use broken and dotted lines to create patterns that evoke movement and rhythm.

Finally, the principle of shape is also evident in the intricate designs of the Yakan cloth. The geometric shapes, such as diamonds, triangles, and squares, create a visual harmony in the cloth. Forms like flowers, fish, and birds are also integrated into the designs, representing the Yakan's connection to nature.

In conclusion, Yakan weaving displays an exceptional use of the elements and principles of art. The skillful combination of colors, lines, shapes, and patterns denotes not only the excellence of the Yakan weavers but also their culture's richness and artistry.    


29. differentiate the attires (color, pattern, and accessories) of the following ethnic groups with one another • Tausug • Yakan • Maranao​


Tausug- They use Muslim motifs called ukkil or okir, which are made up of geometric and flowing arabesque designs.

Male Tausugs wear badju lapi (jacket), sawwal (pants), kandit (sash), and pis siabit (sash) (square piece of cloth tied around the head).

Biyatawi (top), tambuku (gold buttons), and habul tiyahian (bottom) for females (a rectangular cloth draped across the shoulder).

Yakan- Yakans are known for their amazing technicolor geometric weaves and distinctive face decorations, which are used in traditional ceremonies.

Maranao- Tribal and Muslim influences can be seen in the designs and curvilinear motifs, such as stylized vines and the sarimanok.
