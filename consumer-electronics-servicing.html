13. 10 common terminologies used in consumer electronic servicing (need ASAP)​


Terminologies Used in Consumer Electronic Servicing

A technical-vocational curriculum called "Consumer Electronics Servicing" teaches students how to assemble and disassemble consumer electronic systems and products as well as how to maintain and repair audio and video equipment. Terminologies used in consumer electronic servicing:

1. Customer service

The practice of assisting and defending customers while they discover, utilize, optimize, and troubleshoot a product or service is known as customer service. The teams that support them in providing excellent customer service depend on the processes as well.

2. Customer support

The group of employees responsible for helping clients who are having issues with a business's goods or services is known as customer support. Making sure consumers are successful in resolving whatever problems they come to your firm to help with is what matters most in the end.

3. Customer segments

Customer segments are collections of clients with similar needs or traits. The customer base of a business may be segmented based on region, industry, product usage, firm size, revenue, or any other significant differential.

4. Feedback loop

A feedback loop is a system that enables customers to talk to a business about their specific customer service experiences and suggestions for how the business can do better. A closed feedback loop enables the business to gather and analyze customer feedback, make the necessary adjustments, and follow up with the customer directly.

5. Conversational customer service

Customers detest having to repeat themselves, and they don't like being put on hold while an agent looks for information that should be simple to find. We now have conversational customer service, which enables quick, individualized, unbroken service across web, mobile, and social apps.

6. Quality assurance

Monitoring and maintaining a predetermined standard for customer support is the process of quality assurance. This process determines the proper level of service, establishes standards for agents, and aids in performance evaluation.

7. Support operations

The support operation is the collective group in charge of delivering, evaluating, and enhancing customer service. In addition to a general customer service agent, a support operation may also include other job roles such as workforce management, training, quality assurance, customer relationship management, specialist agent, and analyst.

8. Help desk

A help desk is a centralized team within a business that provides mass services to customers or employees while using software to manage conversations.

9. Call center

A call center is a group of managers and sales and customer service representatives who deal with incoming and outgoing communications from potential and current clients.

10. Customer care

Customer care refers to how well a business treats its clients and forges an emotional bond with them. Everyone on the team, not just a customer success manager or a customer service representative, should be able to handle it.
