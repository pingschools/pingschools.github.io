5. What makes the art of the people of Bulacan and Pampanga Filipino? Cite example.


Answer:

Bulacan's Art:

The Art of Making Bulacan Pastillas Wrappers (also known as "Pabalat" or "Borlas de Pastillas") Pastillas come in a variety of shapes and sizes in the Philippines. What makes the pastillas of Bulacan different is how they are wrapped in beautifully constructed pastilla wrappers, also known as pabalat or borlas de pastillas.

Pampanga's Art:

The Kuran ampong Pasu, Pukpuk, Dukit, Parul, Burarul, Dase, Kupia, Santos, Gitara, and Gawang pande are among the ten (10) arts that the province is known for. The above-mentioned arts can be found in various locations of Pampanga. Every art form represents the town or city in which it is most often practiced.

What makes Philippine art Filipino?: The concept was that portraying scenes of everyday life and surroundings without idealizing them was the most spiritually related to the Filipino soul and home soil. (The local magic realists' awareness of genre keeps them from being wholly derivative.)

EXAMPLE OF FILIPINO ART:

Folk architecture, marine transport, notable sculpture in the Philippines, weaving, carving, folk performing arts, folk (oral) literature, folk graphic and plastic arts, ornament, pottery, and other artistic representations of traditional culture are among the traditional arts in the Philippines.

7. why do people calibrate the pulilan carabao festival (pulilan bulacan)​


Answer:

Celebration of the Pulilan Carabao Festival

People from Pulilan, Bulacan celebrate the Kneeling Carabao Festival every May 14 and 15 because they wanted to give thanks to the patron saint of the town, San Isidro Labrador. He is also the patron saint of the farmers who are using the carabaos for their work in the field.

Explanation:

Every May 14 and 15, the townspeople of Pulilan, Bulacan line up the streets to witness the annual Kneeling Carabao Festival. Many tourists from all over the Philippines also come to the town to see what the celebration is all about. The parade starts at 2 PM, in Barangay Dampol 1st, and the carabaos, along with the dancers and the musicians, would have to walk more than 1.5 kilometers just to reach the San Isidro Labrador Parish Church in Barangay Poblacion.

18. Suppose an intensity V earthquake occurs what effect will earthquake have on people structures and environment?​


The severity of an earthquake can be expressed in terms of both intensity and magnitude. However, the two terms are quite different, and they are often confused.

Intensity is based on the observed effects of ground shaking on people, buildings, and natural features. It varies from place to place within the disturbed region depending on the location of the observer with respect to the earthquake epicenter.

Magnitude is related to the amount of seismic energy released at the hypocenter of the earthquake. It is based on the amplitude of the earthquake waves recorded on instruments which have a common calibration. The magnitude of an earthquake is thus represented by a single, instrumentally determined value.
