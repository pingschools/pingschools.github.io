To successfully hold someone liable for malicious mischief, the following elements must be proven: 1) that the offender purposefully caused damage to another's property; 2) that such act does not constitute arson or other crimes involving destruction; and 3) that the act of damaging another's property has been committed solely for the purpose of damaging it.
