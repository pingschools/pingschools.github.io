6. Explain the difference between Factual history and speculative history.​


Answer:

Factual history refers to events, people, and circumstances that can be verified through evidence and documentation. It is based on facts that have been proven to be true through research and analysis.

Speculative history, on the other hand, refers to events, people, and circumstances that are not based on factual evidence, but rather on speculation, conjecture, or theory. It is often used to fill in gaps in historical knowledge and to explore what might have happened in the past. Speculative history can take the form of historical fiction, alternate history, or other forms of creative writing.

12. Explain the difference between factual history & speculative history


Answer:

Factual history refers to a documented and verifiable account of past events that are based on reliable sources such as primary sources (e.g. letters, diaries, government documents, etc.) and secondary sources (e.g. books, articles, and academic papers written by historians and other experts). Factual history is concerned with providing an accurate and objective account of what actually happened in the past.

On the other hand, speculative history refers to accounts of past events that are based on speculation, conjecture, and unverified information. This type of history often involves filling in the gaps in our knowledge about the past with imaginative scenarios or alternative interpretations of events. Speculative history can take many forms, including historical fiction, alternate history, and conspiracy theories. While these narratives can be interesting and entertaining, they should not be taken as fact and should be approached with a critical and skeptical eye.

In summary, factual history aims to be a true and accurate representation of past events, while speculative history is based on speculation and unverified information, and should be treated with caution.
