The first key in the flat family that has one flat is the key of F.

5. Which of the following is correct about the Key of G major?


G MAJOR

The G major is a significant scale dependent on G, with the pitches G, A, B, C, D, E, and F♯. Its key mark has one sharp. Its general minor is E minor and its equal minor is G minor. G major is a melodic key that has G as the tonic. The G significant scale is comprised of 8 notes, isolated by a particular example of entire advances (W) and half advances (H): W-W-H-W-W-W-H, with the notes being G-A-B-C-D-E-F#-G. There have different chords: the group of three harmonies in the key of G major are G major, A minor, B minor, C major, D major, E minor, and F# decreased. At the point when you add sevenths you end up with the four note harmonies, G significant seventh, A minor seventh, B minor seventh, C significant seventh, D predominant seventh, E minor seventh, and F# minor seventh level five. The G Major key uses the scale degree harmonies of the G significant scale, which are G, Am, Bm, C, D, Em, and F#mb5.

27. which of the following is correct about the key of G major?​


Answer:

The key of G major is a musical key that consists of the following notes: G, A, B, C, D, E, and F#.

In G Major key, G is the tonic or the root note, A is the supertonic, B is the mediant, C is the subdominant, D is the dominant, E is the submediant, and F# is the leading tone.

G Major key is a major key, it means that the scale built on G note has a major quality, and it has a bright and joyful character. The chords in G major are G major, A minor, B minor, C major, D major, E minor and F# diminished.

In terms of the circle of fifths, G major is located clockwise from C major.
