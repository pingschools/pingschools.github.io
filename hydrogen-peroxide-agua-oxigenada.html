14. A solution of Agua oxigenada contains 9 ml of hydrogen peroxide in a 300 ml solution. determine the percent by volume of the solution?​


Answer:

Given:

volume of solute (hydrogen peroxide) = 9mL

volume of solution = 300mL

Required:

percent by volume

Formula/Equation:

% by volume = x 100%

 % by volume = [tex]\frac{9 mL}{300 mL}[/tex] x 100%

 % by volume = 0.03 x 100%

% by volume = 3%

Explanation:

A solution is composed of solute that is dissolved in a solvent. The concentration of a solution depends on the amount of solute in a given solution. The concentration of a solution can be unsaturated, saturated, or supersaturated. But, the more specific way to describe concentration is either through percent by mass, or percent by volume depending on what quantities the solute and solution are expressed. But they are the ratio of the amount of solute in relation to the amount of solution, then multiplied by 100%. Thus, the formula in getting the percent y volume is:

     % by volume = [tex]\frac{volume of solute}{volume of solution}[/tex] x 100%

The higher the percent by mass or percent by volume, the more concentrated the solution is.
