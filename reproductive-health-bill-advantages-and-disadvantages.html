21. what is the advantages and disadvantages of sexual reproduction and ansexual reproduction of animal


Most animals reproduce sexually, for example, rabbits. The process of sexual reproduction introduces variation into the species because the alleles that the mother and the father carry are mixed together in the offspring. A disadvantage is that sexual reproduction takes longer than asexual reproduction. A mate must be found, the egg must be fertilised by sperm, and then the offspring develop. The benefit of introducing genetic variation into the species, however, outweighs this disadvantage. If a disease were to hit the rabbit population, then perhaps not all of the rabbits would be affected because of the variation in the population. This means that some individuals would survive and be able to reproduce and generate more offspring.

Advantages of sexual reproduction in animals:

It allows for genetic diversity and adaptability, which can help a species survive in changing environments.

Disadvantages of sexual reproduction in animals:

It requires the presence of both male and female individuals, which can make it more difficult for a species to reproduce in low-density populations or in isolated environments.

It can be more energetically costly than asexual reproduction, as it involves the production and fusion of gametes.

Advantages of asexual reproduction in animals:

It allows a single individual to reproduce without the need for a mate, which can be beneficial in environments where finding a mate is difficult.

It can allow a population to grow quickly, as a single individual can produce many offspring.

It can help a species colonize new environments, as a single individual can give rise to a new population.

Disadvantages of asexual reproduction in animals:

It does not allow for genetic diversity, which can make a population more susceptible to disease and environmental changes.

It can lead to the accumulation of harmful mutations in the population, which can reduce its fitness over time.

26. What are the Advantages and disadvantages of asexual reproduction in plant


ANSWER:

Advantages of sexual reproduction: 
-More genetic variety and so evolves quickly, without relying only on mutations which are often damaging 
-Any negative mutations are likely to be canceled out because the chances both plants have the exact same mutation is very low 

Disadvantages of sexual reproduction: 
-Takes time for pollen to spread, and time is very valuable 
-Often requires an "offering" of nectar to pollinators to encourage them to spread the pollen 

Advantages of asexual reproduction: 
-As soon as a plant is able to reproduce, it can and won't have to wait 
-It can do it by itself and doesn't depend on other species (e.g. pollinators) for survival 

Disadvantages of asexual reproduction: 
-Evolves more slowly and only through DNA mutation, which is most often harmful 
-Disadvantageous mutations usually are passed on to the offspring, instead of having a second "parent" compensate by giving a working copy of the gene
