16. What is the digital counterpart of print media now available to authors and publishers?


The answers is Electronic Publishing also referred to as e-publishing or digital publishing or online publishing. It is a process of converting the paper-based publishing into a digital or electronic format. It does not include using an offset printing  to print the product. It avoids distribution of a physical product, for example paper books, paper magazines, or paper newspapers. It may be distributed over the internet.

The first digitization initiative was in 1971 in United States, by Michael S. Hart. He decided to launch the project named Gutenberg, when he was a student at the Illinois University. The project was about making literature more accessible to everyone, through internet. Electronic publishing is aiming to integrate the whole process of editing and publishing in the digital world. Electronic publishing includes digital publication of e-books, digital magazines, and digital libraries and catalogs. Materials can be read by user using electronic and digital devices, including desktop computers, laptops, tablet computers, smartphones or e-reader tablets. Contents of an electronic file can also be printed on paper.
