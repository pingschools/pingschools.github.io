18. what does slope of distance vs. time graph represent


Distance vs. Time graph represent the speed at which a object is moving away from the starting point.

In Distance vs. Time graph, distance is on the vertical axis or (y-axis) and time is on the horizontal axis or (x-axis). When the object starts moving, time and distance increases. When the moving object stop, only time increases while distance is stationary and you will observe a horizontal straight line in the graph. When the object is moving at a constant speed, you will observe a straight line going northeast. When the object returns to start, you will observe a line graph going downward.

Types of line observed in distance vs time graph

Steeper lineCurving upwardHorizontal lineStraight line

30. what does the slope of a speed vs. time graph represent?​


Answer:

A sloping line on a speed-time graph represents an acceleration . The sloping line shows that the speed of the object is changing. The object is either speeding up or slowing down. If the line slopes upward from left to right, this means the object is speeding up.

The principle is that the slope of the line on a position-time graph is equal to the velocity of the object. If the object is moving with a velocity of +4 m/s, then the slope of the line will be +4 m/s. If the object is moving with a velocity of -8 m/s, then the slope of the line will be -8 m/.
