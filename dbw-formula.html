Answer:

1. To compute for the DBW-F of a regular student with a height of 5'5", we can use the formula:

DBW-F = (Height in inches - 60) x 2.3 + 100

Converting the height of 5'5" to inches, we have:

5 feet = 60 inches

0.5 feet = 6 inches

Total height = 60 + 6 = 66 inches

Substituting the value in the formula:

DBW-F = (66 - 60) x 2.3 + 100

DBW-F = 13.8 + 100

DBW-F = 113.8 lbs (pounds)

To compute for the Total Energy Requirement (TER), we can use the Harris-Benedict equation, which is:

TER = BMR x PAL

where BMR is the basal metabolic rate and PAL is the physical activity level. For a regular student, we can assume a PAL of 1.6-1.7, which corresponds to light to moderate physical activity.

To compute for BMR, we can use the following formula for males:

BMR = 10 x weight (in kg) + 6.25 x height (in cm) - 5 x age + 5

To convert the weight and height from pounds and inches to kilograms and centimeters, respectively:

Weight in kg = weight in lbs / 2.2046

Weight in kg = 113.8 / 2.2046

Weight in kg = 51.63 kg

Height in cm = height in inches x 2.54

Height in cm = 66 x 2.54

Height in cm = 167.64 cm

Assuming a 20-year-old student, we can compute for the BMR:

BMR = 10 x 51.63 + 6.25 x 167.64 - 5 x 20 + 5

BMR = 1076.94 calories/day

Finally, substituting the values in the Harris-Benedict equation:

TER = 1076.94 x 1.6 to 1.7

TER = 1723.1 to 1837.8 calories/day

Therefore, the Total Energy Requirement (TER) of the regular student with a height of 5'5" who performs light to moderate physical activities is approximately 1723 to 1838 calories/day.

2. The target heart rate (THR) range for a 12-year-old student during exercise depends on their age, resting heart rate, and level of physical activity. The general formula for calculating the THR range is:

THR = [(max HR − resting HR) × %Intensity] + resting HR

where max HR is the maximum heart rate, resting HR is the resting heart rate, and %Intensity is the desired exercise intensity (usually 50-85%).

To estimate the maximum heart rate, subtract the student's age from 220. For a 12-year-old student, the estimated maximum heart rate would be:

220 - 12 = 208 beats per minute (bpm)

Assuming a resting heart rate of 70 bpm and a desired exercise intensity of 60%, the THR range would be:

THR = [(208 - 70) × 0.6] + 70

THR = 148.8 bpm

Therefore, the THR range for a 12-year-old student with a resting heart rate of 70 bpm and a desired exercise intensity of 60% is approximately 149 bpm.
