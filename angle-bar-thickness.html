1. A cantilever hollow circular bar is 1.5 m long, 5 mm thick and with outside diameter of 75 mm. It is subjected to a torque of 3 kN.m at its free end. What is the resulting angle of twist (degrees) of the bar. G = 78 GPa.​


Answer:

The resulting angle of twist is 0.077 radians

Explanation:

In this problem, we will use the concept similar to elastic deformation which is the deformation due to torsion like twisting.

The formula to be used is:

a. Φ = [tex]\frac{TL}{JG}[/tex]

b. J = π/32(D⁴ - d⁴)

where

Φ    is the angle of twist, the unit is in radians, rad

T     is the internal torque, the unit is in N·m

L     is the length of the segment, the unit is in meter, m

J      is the polar moment of inertia, the unit is in m⁴

D     is the outside diameter, the unit is in meter, m

d     is the inner diameter, the unit is in meter, m

G     is the shear modulus, the unit is in GPa

Given information

L = 1.5 m

D = 75 mm = 0.075 m

d = 75 mm - 5 mm = 70 mm = 0.070 m

T = 3 kN

G = 78 GPa

Φ = ?

Solving the problem

a. Let us solve first the polar moment of inertia using the 2nd given formula

J = π/32(D⁴ - d⁴)

Then substitute the given data

J = π/32(0.075⁴ - 0.070⁴)

J = 7.49 x 10⁺⁷ m⁴

b. Now, let us solve for the angle of twist using the 1st given formula

Φ = [tex]\frac{TL}{JG}[/tex]

Then substitute the given data

Φ = [tex]\frac{(3x10^3N)(1.5m)}{(7.49x10^{-7})(78x10^9N/m^2)}[/tex]

Φ = 0.077 radians

Therefore, the resulting angle of twist of the bar is 0.077 radians.

Answer:

Step-by-step explanation:

To find the number of soap bars there can be, we must first find the area of the rectangular bar of soap.

[tex]Area_{soap} = lwh \\ Area_{soap} = 5 × 6 × 2 \\ Area_{soap} = 60[/tex]

The area of the soap is 60 cm².

The next thing we do is to find the area of the box.

[tex]Area_{box} = lwh \\ Area_{box} = 50 × 36 × 24 \\ Area_{box} = 43200[/tex]

The area of the box is 43,200 cm².

The last step is to divide the area of the box by the area of a soap bar.

[tex]bars \: of \: soap = \frac{Area_{box}}{Area_{soap}} \\ bars \: of \: soap = \frac{43 200}{60} \\ \blue{bars \: of \: soap = 720}[/tex]

720 soap bars will fit in the box.
